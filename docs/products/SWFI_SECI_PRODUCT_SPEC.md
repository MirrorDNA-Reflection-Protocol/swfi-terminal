# SWFI Sovereign Economic Contribution Index (SECI)

## Position

SECI should be a new SWFI benchmark product, not a tweak to the Linaburg-Maduell Transparency Index.

- LMTI remains the transparency benchmark.
- SECI becomes the contribution benchmark.

## Purpose

Measure how sovereign wealth funds contribute to national economic development, diversification, resilience, and strategic sector growth.

## Strategic Framing

SECI should answer a different question from existing SWFI rankings:

- LMTI asks how transparent a fund is.
- SECI asks how a sovereign fund contributes to its national economy.

This creates a new benchmark category instead of stretching the old one.

## Core Output

Every fund output should include:
- SECI score
- disclosure confidence
- data completeness
- source quality
- last verified
- primary sources used

## Pillars

1. Transparency and disclosure
2. Domestic economic contribution
3. Employment and local capability
4. Strategic sector development
5. Capital mobilization and crowding-in
6. Long-term sustainability and resilience
7. Governance and institutional quality

## Source Hierarchy

1. Audited annual reports
2. Official SWF reports and government disclosures
3. Regulatory filings
4. Multilateral and public-institution sources
5. Credible financial media
6. Derived estimates clearly labeled as such

## Mandate Normalization

SECI should compare funds within mandate classes or show mandate-adjusted scoring across:

- Stabilization Fund
- Savings / Future Generations Fund
- Strategic Development Fund
- Hybrid Fund
- Pension-Reserve Fund
- National Champion Builder

## Required Safeguards

- mandate-adjusted comparison
- source hierarchy
- confidence layer
- no political or causal overclaiming
- no sponsor-controlled scoring

## Key Risks

- attribution risk: contribution indicators are easier to support than direct causality claims
- disclosure bias: more transparent funds are easier to score
- political sensitivity: global sovereign rankings need neutral language
- sponsor independence: methodology ownership must stay with SWFI
- data gaps: low-disclosure funds require confidence bands and insufficiency labels

## First Deliverables

5. sample dashboard and API surface
6. mandate-adjusted pilot scoring set
